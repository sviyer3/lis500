Shrinath Iyer

Who I Am

Hey everyone, my name is Shrinath Iyer and I am a senior attending UW-Madison. I am currently pursuing information sciences and enjoy learning about different facets of information sciences. I enjoy reading, listening to music, and playing sports in my free time, with a keen interest in watching the New England Patriots.

My Academic Interests

I am particularly interested in how artificial intelligence works and it's ability to shape the future. I hope to learn more about artificial intelligence in this class and how it's applications shape the world around us today as well as the underworkings of what really goes into machine learning models. By learning this, I hope to become a more conscientious and passionate developer, becoming a greater tech enthusiast. Machine learning is already behind so many applications so by learning it's background, it will surely help me understand the world as it exists today.

My Goals

I hope to learn more about intersectionality in tech and I am very excited to learn more about all of you as the semester goes on. Furthermore, I want to learn about how bias in technology plays a role in discrimination and what we can do to bridge that gap in order to create a more equitable future. By building such a future, it will allow us to come closer together as people and also create for more society-focused technologies which is what technology should be all about. This class will present me the unique oppurtunity to learn about said bias and by applying it I can do my role in building a better tomorrow.

Yilin Gao

Who I Am

Hi everyone. My name is Yilin Gao. I’m originally from China and I’m currently a senior majoring in Data Science. Growing up and studying in different educational systems has shaped how I think about access, opportunity, and who gets represented in technology. As someone who studies data and algorithms, I’m especially aware that systems are never truly neutral — they reflect the assumptions and priorities of the people who build them.

My Academic Interests

I’m interested in sports and video games, but I also think critically about how platforms shape what we see and experience online. As a data science student, I pay attention to how recommendation systems, ranking systems, and algorithmic decisions can influence what gets promoted, who gets visibility, and what is treated as “normal.” I’m curious about how data can both create opportunity and reinforce inequality.

My Goals

In the long term, I hope to work in technology or data-related fields, but in a way that considers social responsibility. I took this class because I want to better understand how bias enters systems — not only through bad intentions, but through design choices, defaults, and historical data. My goal is to become someone who can build systems more thoughtfully and responsibly.
